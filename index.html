Air Quality Guide
// Reference manual for environmental monitoring parameters
01
What Is Air Quality?
Air quality refers to the condition of the air within our surroundings. Good air quality indicates clean air with minimal pollutants. Poor air quality can cause health problems ranging from minor irritation to serious respiratory conditions.

Air Quality Index (AQI) is the standard metric used worldwide to communicate how polluted the air is. It runs from 0 to 500 — the higher the value, the greater the level of air pollution and the greater the health concern.
02
MQ-2 Gas Sensor
The MQ-2 is a semiconductor gas sensor sensitive to LPG, propane, hydrogen, methane, carbon monoxide, and smoke. It works by measuring changes in electrical resistance of a tin dioxide (SnO₂) sensing element when exposed to target gases.

Resistance decreases as gas concentration increases. The output is an analog voltage converted to PPM (parts per million) by the microcontroller.
Gas Level (PPM)StatusAction
0 – 200CleanNone required
200 – 500ModerateMonitor closely
500 – 1000PoorVentilate area
1000+DangerEvacuate immediately
03
Temperature & Humidity
The DHT11 sensor measures ambient temperature and relative humidity. Temperature and humidity are critical air quality parameters — high humidity promotes mold growth and increases the concentration of certain pollutants. Ideal indoor conditions are 20–25°C at 40–60% relative humidity.

High temperature combined with high humidity significantly reduces thermal comfort and can worsen respiratory conditions.

04
Outdoor AQI Scale
The OpenWeatherMap Air Pollution API returns AQI on a scale of 1–5 following the European Air Quality Index standard, measuring PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, O₃, SO₂, and CO concentrations.
AQILevelHealth Implication
1GoodNo health risk
2FairAcceptable quality
3ModerateSensitive groups affected
4PoorGeneral population affected
5Very PoorSerious health effects
05
ESP32 & IoT Architecture
This system uses an ESP32 microcontroller — a dual-core 240MHz processor with integrated WiFi and Bluetooth. Sensor data is sampled every 2 seconds, displayed on a local OLED, and transmitted to ThingSpeak cloud every 20 seconds via HTTP GET requests.

ThingSpeak stores data in time-series channels. This dashboard fetches data via ThingSpeak's REST API and OpenWeatherMap's API for outdoor comparison, rendering everything client-side with no backend server required.
06
Health Effects of Poor Air
Short-term exposure to elevated gas and particulate levels causes eye irritation, coughing, headaches, and dizziness. Prolonged exposure can lead to chronic respiratory conditions including asthma, bronchitis, and reduced lung function.

Vulnerable groups — children, elderly, and those with pre-existing respiratory or cardiovascular conditions — are significantly more susceptible to the effects of indoor air pollution.
